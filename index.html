How to Use

Getting Started

Login with your Twitch account to chat, see your followed channels, and use your subscribed emotes. You can also browse without logging in.

Features

  • Third-Party Emotes - FFZ, 7TV, and BTTV emotes are fully supported
  • Emote Picker - Tap the smiley icon to browse and search emotes
  • Autocomplete - Type part of an emote name to see suggestions
  • @Mentions - Tap a username in chat to reply to them
  • Ad-Free Video - Enable in Settings if you have Twitch Turbo

Tips

  • Install this app to your home screen for the best experience
  • Type @ to autocomplete usernames from chat
  • Swipe through emote tabs to filter by provider
  • Picture-in-Picture - Press P or tap the PiP button on the video for floating video
  • Use Dark theme in settings for a darker look
  • Set a Sleep Timer to auto-pause the stream

Browser vs Installed App

In a regular browser: If you're logged into Twitch in another tab, the video player will recognize your account. This means no ads if you have Twitch Turbo or are subscribed to the channel.

As an installed app: When you add this to your home screen, it runs in its own window separate from your browser. It can't see your Twitch login from other browser tabs, so the video player won't know you're a subscriber. You'll see ads, but you get a cleaner, app-like experience.

TL;DR: Browser = no ads (if subscribed). Installed app = ads, but nicer interface.
